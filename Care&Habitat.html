About Caring for and Raising an

Exotic Pet

Habitat
Preparation

The enclosure should be
appropriately sized for the species
and the animal's size.

It should be made of safe,
easy-to-clean, well-ventilated,
and durable materials.

Include essential accessories such as
hiding spots, climbing structures,
or water bowls to create a suitable environment.

Proper Nutrition

1

The pet should
receive a diet
that meets
its specific
nutritional needs.

2

Feed the animal
the right amount —
not too much
and not too little.

3

Food must always
be fresh, clean,
and handled
hygienically.

Environmental
Control

Different species require
different temperature ranges.

Maintain appropriate humidity levels
for the species, using misting systems
or occasional water sprays if needed.

Many animals need light exposure
to synthesize vitamin D3,
which is essential for calcium absorption.
